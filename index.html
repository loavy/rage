THE ROOM UNDERNEATH

I survived something I still cannot explain without leaving my own body. People asked why, and I hated them for asking, and hated myself because I was asking too. I was ashamed of being alive. I was ashamed of almost not being. I was ashamed that the room knew before anyone else.

I do not know if I wanted to live. I know I wanted the falling to stop. The void did not become beautiful. It did not become meaningful. It only kept opening underneath every normal sentence.

Tonight, if the floor does not come, maybe a voice can. Maybe that is not hope. Maybe it is enough to not fall alone.
