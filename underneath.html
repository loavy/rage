THE ROOM UNDERNEATH

I do not know if I wanted to live.

I know I wanted the falling to stop.

There is a difference. Nobody taught me the difference, so I carried both sentences in the same mouth and wondered why speaking hurt.

I survived, but I did not return all at once. The body came back first. It blinked, answered, ate when asked, smiled when watched. It became evidence people could point to when they needed proof I was fine.

When they asked why, shame stood between us holding every answer shut.

The room knows. The body knows. The website knows because I put the truth somewhere nobody would click by accident.

I made games out of the things I could not say. I made a login out of lying. I made a void out of time. I made a mascot smile until the smile became the lock.

The void did not end when nobody saw it. It only became quieter.

I used to think being unreachable meant nobody loved me correctly. Sometimes that was true. Sometimes it meant I had built a whole sunny website around the one sentence I was terrified to send.

The sentence was not beautiful. It was not brave. It was small and embarrassing and alive: please notice.

I am ashamed of how much of me became performance. I am ashamed of needing help after teaching everyone I was easy. I am ashamed that survival can look like proof while feeling like evidence.

If you found this, maybe you are not saving me. Maybe you are just sitting near the hole.

Tonight, if the floor does not come, maybe a voice can.

Maybe that is not hope.

Maybe it is enough to not fall alone.
