THE ROOM UNDERNEATH

I survived, but I did not return all at once.

The body kept moving like evidence. The mouth kept making useful sounds. People asked why, and I did not have a clean answer, because I was asking too.

I was ashamed of needing help. Ashamed of being alive. Ashamed of almost not being. Ashamed that the room knew the truth before anyone who loved me did.

I do not know if I wanted to live.

I know I wanted the falling to stop.

There is a difference. Nobody taught me the difference.

The room knows. The body knows. The website knows because I put the truth somewhere nobody would click by accident.

If you found this, maybe you are not saving me. Maybe you are just sitting near the hole.

Tonight, if the floor does not come, maybe a voice can.

Maybe that is not hope.

Maybe it is enough
to not fall alone.
